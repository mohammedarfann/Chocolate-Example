Origin & Sourcing

The Sacred Soil

Our journey begins in the micro-climate volcanic shadow of Mount Tsaratanana in Madagascar. The mineral-dense soil feeds our criollo trees—the rarest variety of cacao in the world, yielding only 5% of global harvests.

"To understand cacao is to understand the breath of the earth. We do not extract flavor; we cultivate its release."

Each single-origin pod is hand-harvested by local farming cooperatives at the exact moment of ripeness. Fermented underneath organic banana leaves, our beans develop complex floral notes before embarking on their journey to our Swiss laboratory.

L'Orfèvrerie

The Art of Tempus Cacao

Creating chocolates of this magnitude requires a slow, ritualistic alchemy where temperature and time align perfectly.

Stage 01

Slow Fire Roasting

We roast our beans over volcanic stone ovens at gentle temperatures. This slow, 4-hour procedure preserves key flavor precursors and yields rich floral notes that standard commercial roasting destroys.

Stage 02

72-Hour Conching

Our chocolate paste is conched in traditional granite rollers for 72 continuous hours. Oxygen streams constantly bubble through, vaporizing unwanted bitter acidity and aerating the chocolate until it achieves a perfect, velvety mouthfeel.

Stage 03

Crystal-V Tempering

To secure the signature glossy look and clean snap, we temperature-temper our chocolate in three stages, forcing the cacao butter to crystallize exclusively into Type-V stable crystals.

Stage 04

Astronomical Detailing

Every single truffle is finalized by hand. Whether receiving a brushing of edible 24k gold, a dusting of black sea salt, or a spray of wild raspberry liquor, our chocolatiers finish each chocolate as a standalone masterpiece.
